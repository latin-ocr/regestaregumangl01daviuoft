Chelsea does not appear in Domesday Book among the Middlesex manors of Westminster

Abbey. Ceroehede or Celched is entered as belonging to Edward of Salisbury
(i. 130 b). Chelsea had become the property of Westminster by the time of Abbot

Gervase (c. 1137—0. 1157), who gave a fraudulent lease of Chelsea to his mother
(Armitage Robinson, Flete's History, p. 89).
